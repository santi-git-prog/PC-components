Procedure

Step 1: Identifying Components

  • Open the computer cabinet using a screwdriver.
  • Identify and list each component inside the cabinet.
  • Ensure the system is powered off and unplugged from the power source.
  • Ground yourself to prevent static discharge that could damage internal components.
  • Remove the screws on the back of the case and gently slide off the side panel.

Step 2: Understanding Connections

  • Locate the connections between the motherboard, CPU, RAM, storage devices, and PSU.
  • Examine how the power cables and data cables are connected.

Step 3: Removing and Reinstalling Components

  • Carefully remove the RAM by unlocking the latches and gently pulling it out.
  • Dismount the storage drive from its bracket or bay.
  • If removing the CPU, first unscrew and detach the cooler.
  • Open the CPU retention lever and carefully lift the CPU from the socket.
  • Reinstall all components, ensuring proper alignment and connections.

Step 4: Reassembling and Testing

  • Close the cabinet and screw the panels back into place.
  • Reconnect all external peripherals such as the keyboard, mouse, and monitor.
  • Plug the power cable back into the PSU and a power source.
  • Power on the system and check if it boots properly.
